What is planned obsolescence?

According to French law, programmed obsolescence is "all techniques, including software, by which the person responsible for placing a product on the market deliberately aims to reduce its lifespan". In 2020 in France only 40% of electrical and electronic appliances that break down are repaired.

Do you want to know the durability of your product?

An act of citizenship? Even more so!

In order to reduce waste, the government aims to increase this proportion to 60% within five years by introducing a reparability index to be displayed in shops. The reparability index is a mandatory note to be displayed on products at the time of purchase. This measure was introduced on 1 January 2021 as part of the anti-waste law promulgated on 10 February 2020, and covers several types of appliance. The aim of this index is to make consumers aware of the need to repair products as soon as they are purchased by informing them of the repairability of their appliance. A score is given by the appliance manufacturer for each of the criteria according to the rating grids defined by the Ministry of Ecological Transition. The final score of the reparability index is an average of the criteria represented by a score out of 10 for the consumer.
